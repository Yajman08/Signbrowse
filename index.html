Indian Sign Language (ISL)

💡
Quick Start: Highlight any text in this browser window, then Right-Click to open the custom menu, or click the floating "🤟 Translate" badge that appears!

Indian Sign Language (ISL) is the predominant sign language used by the deaf community in India. It is a complete and rich natural language with its own grammar, syntax, and vocabulary, entirely distinct from spoken languages like English or Hindi.

Unlike spoken languages which rely on acoustic sounds, ISL relies on visual-spatial communication. This incorporates a combination of hand gestures (handshapes), movement, orientation, facial expressions, and body postures to convey meaning and syntax.

Fingerspelling and Signs

In ISL, spelling out English words letter-by-letter is known as fingerspelling. It is commonly used for proper nouns (names of people, cities, brands) or words for which a specific sign does not yet exist. ISL uses a unique two-handed fingerspelling system, which differs significantly from the one-handed system used in American Sign Language (ASL).

The SignBrowse extension seeks to bridge the gap by parsing page text and generating dynamic sign overlays. This allows users to easily learn or read web content in sign formats.

📖 How to Run Locally in Chrome

To run this project as a real extension inside your Google Chrome browser:

1
Open Chrome and enter chrome://extensions in the address bar.
2
Enable Developer Mode using the toggle switch in the top-right corner.
3
Click the "Load unpacked" button in the top-left corner.
4
Navigate to and select this root folder: SignBrowse.
